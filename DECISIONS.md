42GR Decisions Log

Status:  Living Document (last updated 7/14/2026)

Purpose. This document records significant decisions made during the development of 42GR.

The purpose of this log is to preserve:
* Why decisions were made.
* The strategic principles behind those decisions.
* Which decisions are considered foundational versus still open for discussion.

42GR is being developed through collaboration between expert 42 players and technology partners. Decisions should be challenged, refined, and improved as new insights emerge.

⸻

Project Vision & Philosophy

Decision #001 — 42GR is more than a digital 42 game

Decision:
42GR will be a platform for playing, learning, improving, and preserving the strategic knowledge of 42.

Reason:
Existing digital versions of 42 do not fully address either:

* The need for experienced players to have a realistic strategic challenge.
* The need for new players to have a structured path to learn the game.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #002 — Preserve expert reasoning, not just game rules

Decision:
42GR will capture how expert players think, not merely the mechanics of the game.

Reason:
Expert 42 play depends on:

* Pattern recognition.
* Hand evaluation.
* Partnership understanding.
* Uncertainty management.
* Strategic planning.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #003 — Every feature must strengthen the game

Decision:
Features should only be added if they improve:

* Gameplay.
* Learning.
* Competition.
* Understanding of 42.

Reason:
The product should enhance the game, not distract from it.

Status: Canonical

⸻

AI Philosophy

Decision #004 — AI will never use unavailable information

Decision:
AI players must make decisions using only information available to a human player in the same position.

Reason:
42GR must provide fair competition and maintain player trust.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #005 — AI reasoning should come from the Canon

Decision:
The AI should reason from documented expert principles rather than simply relying on brute-force simulation or statistical patterns.

Reason:
The goal is not only to produce correct decisions, but explainable decisions.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #006 — Every important AI decision should have an explanation

Decision:
The system should be able to answer:

“Why?”

Reason:
The explanation is the bridge between playing 42 and learning 42.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Product Architecture

Decision #007 — 42GR will consist of four major components

Decision:
The long-term product architecture will contain:

1. The Canon
2. The Reasoning Engine
3. The Game Engine
4. The Mentor Engine

Reason:
Separating knowledge, reasoning, mechanics, and coaching allows the system to evolve.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #008 — The Canon must be developed before advanced AI

Decision:
Expert knowledge must be documented before being translated into software.

Reason:
Without a knowledge foundation, the AI may make moves without understanding the principles behind them.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #009 — The Mentor Engine is a core feature

Decision:
Coaching is not an optional feature. It is a central part of 42GR.

Reason:
The ability to teach new players is one of the project’s primary goals.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Strategy Engine

Decision #010 — Hand recognition comes before bidding

Decision:
The first strategic task is identifying what type of hand has been received.

Examples:

* Trump hand.
* Follow Me hand.
* Nello hand.
* Splash opportunity.
* Plunge opportunity.
* Defensive hand.

Reason:
Expert players identify the nature of the hand before deciding what to bid.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #011 — Bidding is contract selection

Decision:
The system should first determine the best strategic approach, then determine the appropriate bid.

Reason:
Different contracts require different objectives and strategies.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #012 — Each contract requires its own strategy framework

Decision:
Trump, Mark, Nello, Splash, Plunge, and defensive play will each have unique reasoning models.

Reason:
The goal of each contract changes the correct strategy.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Strategic Principles

Decision #013 — Control is a means, not the final objective

Decision:
Maintaining control exists to create opportunities to accomplish the contract objective.

Reason:
Expert play is not simply about winning tricks. It is about converting control into success.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #014 — Pulling Count is a foundational concept

Decision:
“Pulling Count” is the accepted term for deliberately creating opportunities to capture count dominoes.

Reason:
This accurately describes a core element of expert offensive play.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #015 — Count has different value depending on the contract

Decision:

For non-mark bids:

* Count acquisition is critical.

For mark bids:

* Winning all tricks is the primary objective.

For Nello:

* Avoiding tricks is the objective.

Reason:
The value of count depends on the contract being played.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #016 — Domino value is contextual

Decision:
A domino does not have a fixed value independent of the hand.

Reason:
Value depends on:

* Trump selection.
* Contract.
* Position.
* Remaining dominoes.
* Partnership situation.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #017 — Structural risk must be evaluated

Decision:
Hand evaluation must consider weaknesses and vulnerabilities, not just strengths.

Examples:

* Weak offs.
* Loss of control.
* Exposed count.
* Poor endgame position.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Partnership

Decision #018 — Partnership reasoning is central

Decision:
42GR must model partnership strategy, not just individual hand strength.

Reason:
42 is fundamentally a partnership game.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #019 — Different expert styles should be preserved

Decision:
The system should recognize that strong players may reach different conclusions using the same principles.

Reason:
The goal is not to eliminate style, but to understand expert reasoning.

Status: Proposed

⸻

Development Philosophy

Decision #020 — Expert players are knowledge contributors

Decision:
Wayne, Rodney, Jerrand, and other experienced players are contributors to the Canon, not simply testers.

Reason:
Their experience is the foundation of the system.

Status: Canonical

⸻

Decision #021 — The Canon should be challenged

Decision:
Disagreement between expert players is valuable.

Reason:
Differences reveal deeper principles and prevent the system from capturing only one person’s style.

Status: Canonical
